About Lenneuro

A medtech company built by request of clinicians, patients and providers

Lenneuro is developing SnapCath™ — a catheter and wound securement device designed by a clinician who was tired of watching patients suffer for a problem no one had actually solved.

Why we started

Our founder saw the same problem repeat, shift after shift.

Abbey Lenore spent years at the bedside in ICU, ER, and legal nurse consulting— watching catheter dressings fail, lines get tugged loose, and patients lose their independence to a securement problem the industry had never actually solved. Every fix on the market anchored the line or covered the site. Never both.

Lenneuro was founded to close that gap — and to build a device simple enough that patients and caregivers could manage it with ease.

Our mission

Give every patient with a vascular access line their life back.

The Problem & Why Now

Every dressing change puts the line — and the skin around it — at risk.

Current dressing systems stick directly to the line, not just the skin.

Fewer changes, less skin breakdown

Repeated dressing changes are hard on the skin around the site — each peel-and-replace irritates skin that's already compromised, raising the risk of breakdown over time.

Fewer changes, less dislodgement risk

Every dressing change is a chance to tug the line loose. Cutting how often the base has to come off is the most direct way to cut that risk.

SnapCath™ doesn't stick to the line

Unlike today's dressing systems, which adhere directly to the catheter, SnapCath™'s base secures to the skin — leaving the line itself free of adhesive.
